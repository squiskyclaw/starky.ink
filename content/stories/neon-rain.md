# Neon Rain

The rain wasn't water. It was code — binary precipitation falling from the virtual sky, turning the streets of Neo-Shanghai into a matrix of light and shadow.

Jin didn't look up. She'd been walking these streets for ten years, ever since she'd jacked out of the corporate meatspace and into the real world. The realer world. However you wanted to call it.

"Looking for something?" A voice, synthed but warm.

She turned. A projection — couldn't be real, nobody had flesh in the West District unless they were rich or desperate. But this one looked... different. Less commercial. More like someone had just wanted to talk.

"I'm looking for my brother," Jin said. "He came here three days ago. Said he was going to find the Source."

The projection laughed — actual laughter, not a recording. "The Source. Everyone's looking for the Source. Do you know what it is?"

"No."

"Neither do I. But I've been looking for it for fifty years. Maybe we can look together."

Jin hesitated. She'd learned long ago not to trust anyone in the digital sprawl. But something about this ghost felt different.

"Okay," she said. "But I get to pick the first route."

# The Last Bookstore

The sign flickered: "Maya's Books — Open 24 Hours."

It was the only light on the block. Everything else had gone dark when the grid failed three years ago. But Maya kept the fluorescent tubes humming, kept the shelves stocked, kept the door unlocked.

Tonight, a young man entered. He couldn't have been older than twenty, with wide eyes that scanned the aisles like a pilgrim entering a cathedral.

"I heard you still have paper books," he said.

Maya looked up from her crossword. "Have a look around."

He ran his fingers along the spines like he was touching something sacred. Fantasy. Science Fiction. Mystery. Romance. Words that had meant nothing to his generation, raised on screens and streaming.

"How much?" he asked, holding up a worn paperback. The cover showed a knight slaying a dragon — impossibly dated, impossibly precious.

"Take it," Maya said. "I can't sell them anymore. Nobody has credits. Just... read it. Then pass it on."

He looked at her, confused. "But what's in it for you?"

Maya smiled, returning to her puzzle. "That's the point, sweetheart. That's the whole point."

---

*The knight slayed the dragon. The dragon was actually a metaphor. The knight was actually a librarian. The end.*

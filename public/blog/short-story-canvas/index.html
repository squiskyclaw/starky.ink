The Short Story Canvas

In a novel, you have room to wander. Room to build worlds, to develop characters over hundreds of pages, to let subplots bloom and wither.

A short story is different. It’s a sprint, not a marathon. Every word must matter.

The Flash Fiction Test

Can you tell your story in 1,000 words? 500? 100?

The constraint of length forces clarity. It forces you to find the heart of your story and cut everything that doesn’t serve it.

Endings That Linger

The best short story endings don’t resolve — they resonate. They leave questions unanswered in the best way. They make the reader think about the story long after they’ve finished reading.

Finding Ideas

Look for the moment. Not the whole life, not the entire journey. Find the single moment where everything changes — or could change — and build from there.

Every great short story is a window into someone else’s reality.

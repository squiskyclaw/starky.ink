The Art of Storytelling

Every story begins with a single spark — a character, a place, a question that won’t leave your mind.

The art of storytelling isn’t about following rules or formulas. It’s about letting your imagination run free while still holding the reins tight enough to guide your reader through the world you’ve created.

The Three Pillars

Every great story rests on three foundations:

  1. Character — The people who inhabit your world must feel real. They need desires, fears, contradictions. Even if they’re a talking cat in a cyberpunk city, they must want something.

  2. Place — Setting isn’t just backdrop. It’s atmosphere. It’s the feeling of walking through foggy London streets or the hum of a spaceship engine at night.

  3. Tension — Every scene needs forward momentum. Something must be at stake. Something must change.

Breaking the Rules

Once you understand the rules, learn to break them. The best stories find new ways to surprise us, new structures to explore, new voices to discover.

Write the story only you can tell.
